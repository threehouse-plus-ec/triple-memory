# Phase 3 Local Display Curation (TC-32)

**Status:** Cross-checked per TC-32. Ready for TC-33 (JSON merge).

This document defines the `local_display` forms for the 8 pilot entries. Per the Blueprint §9.2, this is the most widely used official local form in Latin script. Multilingual states include a `language_note`.

## Entries
- **AUT (Austria):** Österreich (Country) · Wien (Capital) · Donau (River)
- **BEL (Belgium):** België / Belgique (Country) · Brussel / Bruxelles (Capital) · Maas / Meuse (River)
  - *Language Note:* Belgium is highly multilingual. Dutch and French official forms are presented together to reflect federal bilingual equality at the national level.
- **DEU (Germany):** Deutschland (Country) · Berlin (Capital) · Rhein (River)
- **ESP (Spain):** España (Country) · Madrid (Capital) · Tajo (River)
- **ITA (Italy):** Italia (Country) · Roma (Capital) · Po (River)
- **NLD (Netherlands):** Nederland (Country) · Amsterdam (Capital) [Alt: Den Haag] · Rijn (River)
- **EGY (Egypt):** Miṣr (Country) · Al-Qāhirah (Capital) · An-Nīl (River)
  - *Language Note:* Arabic is the official language. Rendered in standard Latin romanisation per MVP conventions.
- **ZAF (South Africa):** Suid-Afrika / iNingizimu Afrika (Country) · Pretoria (Capital) [Alt: Kaapstad, Bloemfontein] · Oranjerivier (River)
  - *Language Note:* South Africa has 12 official languages. Afrikaans and Zulu are widely used alongside English. The most common alternative Latin-script forms are provided here as a representative baseline.
